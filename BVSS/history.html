THE BVSS HISTORY

           Sajjan Rao was born and brought up in an humble environment in a small village near Maddur. His maternal uncle Venkata Rao Bhojgade brought him Bengaluru to earn livelihood. The shrewd and smart young boy, quickly learnt the art of trade while helping his mentor.

           He had taken agency of Binny Mill products and was also a sole distributor for all the products of K.R Mills to Mysore Province. He had a dyeing unit in Visvesvarpuram. His close friend and a prominent businessman of Madras, Gokuldas Jamunadas used to import high quility colouring material and send them to Sajjan Rao. When they could not import such materials during World War, the unit was closed.

           With the welfare of his Maratha community in mind, he was large;y responsiblle for the growth of Maratha Students Free Boarding Home which was established in 1909. With the nucleus fund of 40,000 rupees provided by the wife of late Venkata Rao Bhojgade, he prudently managed the hostel and its assets, adding his own handsome donation of 50,000 rupees. Hence the name, B. Venkata Rao S. Sajjan Rao Maratha Student's Home. The old graceful building of the hostel in the vast premises on Bull Temple Road, Basavangudi, is still retained. One of the group photos with the managing commitee members and the students was taken in 1925, almost 95 years ago, is preserved well in hostel. It shows S. Sajjan Rao sitting in capacity of the treasurer of the institution.
